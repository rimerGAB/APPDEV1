##  Purpose

Smart glasses are wearable devices that combine traditional eyewear with Artificial Intelligence (AI), Augmented Reality (AR), cameras, microphones, speakers, sensors, and wireless connectivity. Their primary purpose is to provide users with hands-free access to digital information while allowing them to remain aware of their surroundings.

These devices can perform tasks such as real-time navigation, language translation, object recognition, voice assistance, video recording, and information display. In education and the Information Technology (IT) industry, smart glasses improve accessibility, productivity, collaboration, and learning by providing contextual information directly within the user's field of view ***(All About Vision, 2026; Jisc National Centre for AI in Tertiary Education, 2026).***

## Usage

### In Education

Smart glasses improve accessibility and support practical learning experiences. They assist students with visual impairments by reading printed text aloud, recognizing faces, identifying objects, and describing their surroundings.

They are also used during laboratory activities and technical demonstrations by displaying instructions directly within the user's field of view, allowing students to focus on practical tasks while receiving real-time guidance ***(Envision, n.d.; Jisc National Centre for AI in Tertiary Education, 2026; Times Higher Education, 2026)***.

### In the IT Industry

The Information Technology (IT) industry requires professionals to troubleshoot hardware, maintain networks, configure systems, and provide technical support. These responsibilities often require technicians to switch between physical equipment and digital documentation.

Smart glasses improve this workflow by allowing technicians to access manuals, diagnostics, system information, and remote assistance directly within their field of view while keeping both hands available for technical work. They are commonly used in network administration, hardware maintenance, cybersecurity monitoring, software testing, and enterprise technical support. By reducing workflow interruptions and enabling real-time collaboration, smart glasses help increase productivity and improve troubleshooting efficiency ***(Viewpointsystem, 2025; Microsoft, n.d.).***

### One Example in Education

**Envision Glasses**

Envision Glasses are AI-powered smart glasses that assist individuals who are blind or have low vision. They can read printed text aloud, recognize faces, identify objects, and describe the surrounding environment. In educational settings, these features help students independently access books, classroom materials, and campus signage, promoting greater accessibility and participation ***(Envision, n.d.; Pacific Vision, 2024)***.

### One Example in the IT Industry

**Microsoft HoloLens 2**

Microsoft HoloLens 2 is an augmented reality headset widely used in enterprise IT environments. It allows technicians to view digital manuals, technical documentation, and interactive 3D models while working on physical equipment. Through Microsoft Dynamics 365 Remote Assist, experts can provide live guidance to field technicians, improving maintenance efficiency and reducing equipment downtime ***(Microsoft, n.d.)***.
